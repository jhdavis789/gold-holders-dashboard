Data sources

Every quantitative value on the dashboard is scripted from the primary sources below — no numbers are supplied by a model. Hover any figure in the dashboard for its specific source, year and method.

Rolling-returns & correlations tabs

Each series is reduced to an annual-average level index; the box-and-whisker plots overlapping N-year (5/10/20y) annualised total returns; the correlation matrix uses annual total returns. Real = deflated by Shiller annual-average CPI. Windows start ≥ 1901, end ≤ 2025. The Period slider clips the sample client-side (handles held ≥ 20 years apart).

Gold-holdings tabs (Snapshot & Trends)

Central-bank reserves

  • World Gold Council — Gold Reserves by Country (underlying IMF IFS). Quarterly tonnage, value, % of reserves, 2000-Q1+ (123 countries).
  • IMF — International Liquidity (IFS). Annual gold-reserve volume back to 1950 (187 countries + World aggregate) — drives the 50-year / Max trend windows.
  • World Bank — Commodity Price Data ("Pink Sheet"), annual gold price 1960+ ($35/oz flat 1950–59).
  • World Bank — GDP (current US$) for the % of GDP metric.

Private / household gold stock (estimates)

  • World Gold Council — above-ground stocks & market size (India ~25,000t; world private ≈ 144,000t).
  • Steinbeis University / Reisebank — Germany private stock survey (9,089t).
  • WGC US Gold Demand Trends + 10.8% ownership survey — US private-stock triangulation.
  • Italy (Kitco), Turkey (Türkiye Today / WGC), France & Switzerland (GoldBroker), Vietnam (National Assembly), China (analyst compilations).

Household wealth & income (context metrics)

  • UBS Global Wealth Databook — household wealth (% of wealth metric).
  • World Bank GNI per capita (Atlas) + OECD median disposable income.

Current gold spot: Yahoo Finance COMEX front-month (GC=F).

Wealth-share tab

Gold's market cap vs world equity market cap, total financial assets, and household net wealth.

  • World Gold Council — above-ground gold stocks (1990+); pre-1990 back-cast from USGS / Schmitz historical mine-production residuals.
  • LBMA Gold Price PM annual average (1969+); Bretton Woods official $35 (1934–68); gold-standard official $20.67 (pre-1934).
  • World Bank — world equity market cap (CM.MKT.LCAP.CD) 1975–2023, extended with WFE end-2024/2025 totals.
  • FSB Global Monitoring Report — total financial assets (banks + insurance + pensions + OFIs), 2014+; pre-2014 values are interpolated anchors vs IMF GFSR / BIS composites, and 2024–25 are extrapolated pending the next GMR.
  • Credit Suisse / UBS Global Wealth Databook & Report — household net wealth (CS GWD 2010 for 2000–2010, UBS GWD 2023, UBS GWR 2024/2025).

Replication / proxy evaluations — do they carry forward?

Several series are artificial replications because the investable index starts too late for long windows: US small-cap & value use academic Ken French factor portfolios (vs Russell, which began 1979/94), the US Aggregate Bond pre-1986 is a synthetic gov/credit blend, and the 10y Treasury is reconstructed from the yield. (IG corporate is not a replication — it is actual realized SBBI index returns; its row below compares the gross high-grade index against an investable net-of-fee fund, where the small positive gap is fees + quality mix, not construction bias.) The test for each replication: run it over the era its investable index does exist and measure the fit on annual total returns. Every fit below — correlation, tracking error, and CAGR gap — is computed by live, auditable Excel formulas in the workbook above (difference column → STDEV, gross-return columns → GEOMEAN).
